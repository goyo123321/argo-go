About WHO

The World Health Organization (WHO), established on April 7, 1948, is a specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health. Headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland, WHO works worldwide to promote health, keep the world safe, and serve the vulnerable.

WHO's primary role is to direct and coordinate international health within the United Nations system. Its main areas of work include health systems, health through the life-course, noncommunicable and communicable diseases, preparedness, surveillance and response, and corporate services.

"Our goal is to build a better, healthier future for people all over the world. Through offices in more than 150 countries, WHO staff work side by side with governments and other partners to ensure the highest attainable level of health for all people."

Our Mission

WHO's mission is to promote health, keep the world safe, and serve the vulnerable. We strive to ensure that a billion more people have universal health coverage, a billion more people are protected from health emergencies, and a billion more people enjoy better health and well-being.

Promote Health

Advancing universal health coverage, addressing social determinants, and creating healthy environments to promote physical and mental health.

Keep the World Safe

Strengthening global health security by preventing, detecting, and responding to public health emergencies and outbreaks.

Serve the Vulnerable

Prioritizing the needs of the most vulnerable populations and ensuring equitable access to essential health services.

WHO History

Since its inception in 1948, WHO has played a crucial role in global health achievements. Here are some key milestones:

1948: WHO Established

The World Health Organization Constitution came into force on April 7, 1948 - a date we now celebrate every year as World Health Day.

1979: Smallpox Eradicated

WHO certified the global eradication of smallpox in 1979, the first disease in history to be eliminated by human effort.

1988: Global Polio Eradication Initiative

WHO launched the Global Polio Eradication Initiative, reducing polio cases by over 99% worldwide.

2003: Framework Convention on Tobacco Control

WHO adopted the first global public health treaty, the Framework Convention on Tobacco Control, to reduce tobacco-related deaths.

2020: COVID-19 Pandemic Response

WHO coordinated the global response to the COVID-19 pandemic, providing guidance, technical support, and facilitating vaccine distribution.

Key Areas of Work

WHO works across a broad range of health issues to achieve better health for everyone, everywhere. Our key areas of focus include:

Communicable Diseases

Preventing and controlling the spread of infectious diseases like HIV, tuberculosis, malaria, and COVID-19 through vaccination, treatment, and surveillance.

Health Systems

Strengthening health systems to achieve universal health coverage and ensure access to quality essential health services.

Noncommunicable Diseases

Preventing and controlling noncommunicable diseases like cancer, diabetes, and heart disease through promotion of healthy lifestyles.

Life Course

Promoting health through all stages of life, from maternal and newborn health to healthy aging.

Health & Environment

Addressing environmental risks to health, including climate change, air pollution, and water and sanitation issues.

Health Emergencies

Preparing for, detecting, and rapidly responding to health emergencies and humanitarian crises.
